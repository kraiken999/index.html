Abstract & Core Equation

Flat sheets in $\mathbb{R}^2$ with crease patterns and signed fold angles are mapped into folded modules in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and assembled by rigid motions. The global configuration

$$ C(t) = \bigcup_{i=1}^N T_i(t)\big(F_i(t)(P_i)\big) $$

provides a universal representation of rigid modular origami and a geometric model of the universe. Quantum-origami configurations are dense in the space of compact, connected, piecewise-smooth surfaces under the Hausdorff metric, and the crease-density field $P_{\text{crease}}(x)$ plays the role of curvature, energy, mass, and gravity.

Standard Model from Fold Motifs

Each module $M_i$ with angle vector $\Theta_i$ is interpreted as a particle-like motif. Define

$$ Q_i = \sum_m \mathrm{sgn}(\Theta_{i,m}), \qquad M_i \propto \sum_m k_m |\Theta_{i,m}|. $$

Charge comes from the signs of the folds; mass comes from how sharply the folds bend. The core configuration $C(t)$ plus the motif map $(\Theta_i \mapsto Q_i, M_i)$ generates the Standard Model table below.
