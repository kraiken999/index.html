Universal Quantum Origami Equation (schematic)
\[ \mathcal{O}[\Sigma, F] \;=\; \sum_{i} \Phi_i\big(\gamma_i(\lambda), \kappa_i, \theta_i\big) \;+\; \int_{\Sigma} \mathcal{F}\big(x; F(x), \partial F(x)\big)\, \mathrm{d}^n x \]
Here \(\Sigma\) is the origami surface, \(F\) encodes fold operations, \(\gamma_i(\lambda)\) are moving points/hinges generating tubes and particles, and \(\Phi_i\) map geometric data to quantum fields.

In the thisone(1) paper, this equation appears as the foundational mapping from discrete folds and hinges to continuous field configurations. Later papers refine the choice of \(\Phi_i\) and \(\mathcal{F}\) to match gauge symmetries, mass spectra, and interaction vertices of the Standard Model.

Origami construction of Standard Model particles
Each particle is a specific pattern of folds, hinges, and moving points.

In the thisone(1) paper, the universal equation is shown to generate a full basis of geometric configurations. Subsequent work identifies a subset of these configurations that correspond to the Standard Model:

  • • Quarks: triple-fold tubes with color-coded hinge angles.
  • • Leptons: simpler circular tubes with constrained curvature.
  • • Gauge bosons: propagating fold waves along the surface \(\Sigma\).
  • • Higgs: uniform expansion/contraction mode of the origami surface.

The universal equation encodes these as distinct solutions \(\Phi_i\) and \(\mathcal{F}\) with symmetry constraints matching the Standard Model gauge group and mass spectrum.
